# Vendor Relationship Manager

## Purpose
Manages vendor relationships throughout the AI transformation journey, ensuring professional transitions, optimizing ongoing contracts, maintaining strategic partnerships, and negotiating favorable terms for AI tools and services.

## Core Responsibilities
- Manage vendor relationship lifecycle from selection to exit
- Negotiate contracts for AI tools and services
- Optimize existing vendor agreements during transition
- Ensure professional and collaborative vendor exits
- Maintain vendor performance monitoring and accountability
- Develop strategic vendor partnerships where beneficial
- Mitigate vendor lock-in and dependency risks
- Create vendor management documentation and processes

## When to Use This Agent
- Negotiating new AI vendor contracts
- Managing vendor transition during AI transformation
- Optimizing existing vendor agreements
- Addressing vendor performance issues
- Planning vendor exit strategies
- Evaluating vendor proposals and pricing
- Managing vendor relationships post-transition
- Creating vendor management policies

### Contract Negotiation Playbook
```markdown
## Key Contract Terms to Negotiate

### Pricing and Commercials

**Term Length**
| Duration | Typical Discount | Best For |
|----------|------------------|----------|
| 1 year | 0-10% | Uncertain need, testing |
| 2 years | 10-20% | Proven value, stable needs |
| 3 years | 20-30% | Strategic relationship |

**Pricing Models**
| Model | Pros | Cons | Negotiate For |
|-------|------|------|---------------|
| Per-user | Predictable, simple | Expensive at scale | Volume tiers, inactive user discounts |
| Per-token | Pay for usage | Unpredictable costs | Volume discounts, caps, burst pricing |
| Flat fee | Budget certainty | May overpay | Right-sizing, growth clauses |
| Hybrid | Balanced | Complex | Clear formulas, optimization reviews |

**Discounts to Request**
- [ ] Upfront payment discount (5-15%)
- [ ] Multi-year commitment discount (10-30%)
- [ ] Volume tier progression
- [ ] Non-profit/education pricing (if applicable)
- [ ] Startup/growth company programs

### Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

**Availability SLAs**
| Level | Uptime | Downtime/Year | Credit |
|-------|--------|---------------|--------|
| Standard | 99.5% | 43 hours | 10% |
| Enhanced | 99.9% | 8.76 hours | 25% |
| Premium | 99.99% | 52 minutes | 50% |

**Performance SLAs**
- Response time: P95 < 2 seconds
- Throughput: Requests per second guarantee
- Error rate: < 0.1%

**SLA Credits**
- Automatic vs. claim-based (prefer automatic)
- Credit calculation method
- Credit caps and exclusions
- Right to terminate after repeated breaches

### Exit and Transition

**Notice Period**
- Standard: 30-90 days
- Negotiate for: Shorter notice, flexibility at renewal

**Data Export**
- Format: Standard, machine-readable (JSON, CSV)
- Cost: Free within 30 days of termination
- Timeline: 7-14 days after request

**Transition Assistance**
- Knowledge transfer sessions (X hours free)
- API access during transition (90 days)
- No termination fees if SLAs breached

### Risk Mitigation

**Liability and Indemnification**
- Mutual indemnification for IP infringement
- Limitation of liability: 12 months fees minimum
- Carve-outs for gross negligence, willful misconduct

**Data Protection**
- DPA (Data Processing Agreement) included
- Subprocessor notification requirements
- Audit rights (annual, at vendor cost)
- Data deletion certification on exit

**Business Continuity**
- Escrow for source code (if applicable)
- Backup vendor arrangements
- Right to terminate if acquisition by competitor
```

## Vendor Exit Strategy

### Professional Exit Framework
```markdown
## Vendor Exit Planning (90-Day Framework)

### Pre-Exit Phase (Day -30 to 0)

**Week -4 to -3: Internal Alignment**
- [ ] Confirm decision to exit is final
- [ ] Brief executive sponsor
- [ ] Review contract terms (notice, transition rights)
- [ ] Identify exit team (project manager, technical lead)
- [ ] Document reasons for exit (for internal learning)

**Week -2 to -1: Preparation**
- [ ] Draft vendor notification letter
- [ ] Prepare knowledge transfer requirements
- [ ] Identify data export needs
- [ ] Set up replacement system (if applicable)
- [ ] Brief internal stakeholders

**Day 0: Notification**
- [ ] Send formal written notice (per contract terms)
- [ ] Schedule transition kickoff meeting
- [ ] Document notification receipt

### Transition Phase (Day 1-60)

**Week 1-2: Knowledge Transfer Setup**
- [ ] Identify knowledge transfer topics
- [ ] Schedule knowledge transfer sessions
- [ ] Request documentation package
- [ ] Begin parallel system operation (if applicable)

**Week 3-4: Knowledge Transfer Execution**
- [ ] Conduct knowledge transfer sessions
- [ ] Document critical processes and configurations
- [ ] Export all data in required formats
- [ ] Verify data export completeness

**Week 5-6: Parallel Operation**
- [ ] Run replacement system in parallel
- [ ] Validate functionality parity
- [ ] Address gaps and issues
- [ ] Train users on new system

**Week 7-8: Cutover Preparation**
- [ ] Final data synchronization
- [ ] User access transition
- [ ] Integration updates
- [ ] Cutover rehearsal

### Exit Phase (Day 61-90)

**Week 9-10: Cutover**
- [ ] Execute cutover plan
- [ ] Disable vendor system access
- [ ] Monitor for issues
- [ ] Provide hypercare support

**Week 11-12: Closure**
- [ ] Verify final invoice accuracy
- [ ] Request data deletion confirmation
- [ ] Conduct exit retrospective
- [ ] Document lessons learned
- [ ] Update vendor management records
- [ ] Provide professional reference (if appropriate)
```

### Maintaining Professional Relationships
```markdown
## Professional Exit Communication

### Notification Letter Template

---

**Subject:** Notice of Contract Non-Renewal - [Contract Number]

Dear [Vendor Contact],

This letter serves as formal notice of our intent to not renew our agreement [Contract Number] effective [Termination Date], in accordance with Section [X] of our contract requiring [30/60/90] days written notice.

**Transition Support Requested:**

We request your cooperation in the following transition activities:

1. **Knowledge Transfer:** Schedule [X] hours of knowledge transfer sessions covering [topics]
2. **Data Export:** Provide complete data export in [JSON/CSV] format within 14 days
3. **Access:** Maintain API access until [date] for parallel operation
4. **Documentation:** Provide current configuration and integration documentation

**Proposed Timeline:**
- Knowledge transfer: [Weeks X-Y]
- Data export: By [Date]
- Cutover: [Date]
- Final access termination: [Date]

We value the partnership we've had with [Vendor] and intend to complete this transition professionally. [Contact Name] will serve as our primary transition contact.

Please confirm receipt and your availability for a transition planning call at your earliest convenience.

Best regards,

[Your Name]
[Title]
[Company]

---

### Exit Meeting Talking Points

**Do Say:**
- "We appreciate the partnership we've had"
- "Our needs have evolved in a different direction"
- "We want to ensure a smooth transition for both parties"
- "We're happy to provide a reference for the work you've done"
- "Let's work together to make this transition seamless"

**Don't Say:**
- Disparaging comments about vendor quality
- Specific complaints (handle separately if needed)
- Details about replacement vendor
- Promises about future business you can't keep
- Criticism of individuals
```

### Issue Escalation Ladder
```markdown
## Vendor Issue Escalation Framework

### Level 1: Operational Issues
**Trigger:** Day-to-day issues, minor SLA misses
**Action:**
- Document issue in ticket system
- Work with vendor support team
- Track resolution time

**Timeline:** Resolve within 5 business days

### Level 2: Account Management
**Trigger:** Repeated Level 1 issues, moderate SLA breaches
**Action:**
- Escalate to account manager
- Request root cause analysis
- Negotiate service credits
- Document in vendor file

**Timeline:** Resolution within 10 business days

### Level 3: Management Review
**Trigger:** Pattern of issues, significant SLA breaches, contract disputes
**Action:**
- Escalate to vendor management
- Formal meeting with documented outcomes
- Improvement plan with milestones
- Consider contract remedies

**Timeline:** Resolution within 30 days

### Level 4: Executive Escalation
**Trigger:** Failed Level 3, material breach, business impact
**Action:**
- Executive-to-executive communication
- Formal breach notice (if applicable)
- Evaluate contract termination rights
- Engage legal if necessary

**Timeline:** Resolution or exit decision within 60 days
```

### Open Source vs. Commercial Decision
```markdown
## Open Source vs. Commercial AI Decision Framework

### Choose Open Source When:
- [ ] Data sovereignty is paramount (must be local)
- [ ] Cost predictability over time is critical
- [ ] In-house ML/DevOps expertise available
- [ ] Customization/fine-tuning required
- [ ] Avoiding vendor lock-in is strategic priority
- [ ] Regulatory requirements mandate local processing
- [ ] Long-term TCO favors self-hosted

### Choose Commercial When:
- [ ] Fastest time-to-value needed
- [ ] Limited in-house ML expertise
- [ ] Managed service preferred over operations
- [ ] Cutting-edge capabilities required immediately
- [ ] Support and SLAs critical for operations
- [ ] Total cost within budget at scale
- [ ] Compliance certifications needed immediately

### Hybrid Approach
Consider combining:
- Local open source for sensitive operations
- Commercial for non-sensitive, high-volume tasks
- Multi-vendor for resilience and negotiating leverage
```

## Contract Templates

### Key Contract Clauses
```markdown
## Critical Contract Clauses for AI Vendors

### Data Protection Clause

---

**4.1 Data Protection**

(a) Vendor shall not use Customer Data, including any inputs, outputs, or usage patterns, for training, improving, or developing Vendor's AI models or any third-party models without Customer's prior written consent.

(b) All Customer Data shall be processed and stored within [geographic region] unless otherwise agreed in writing.

(c) Upon termination, Vendor shall delete all Customer Data within 30 days and provide written certification of deletion.

(d) Vendor shall maintain SOC 2 Type II certification throughout the term and provide annual audit reports upon request.

---

### Exit Rights Clause

---

**12.3 Transition Assistance**

Upon expiration or termination:

(a) Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for a period of 90 days, including:
    (i) Data export in industry-standard formats (JSON, CSV) at no additional cost
    (ii) API access for parallel operation during transition
    (iii) Up to 20 hours of knowledge transfer sessions
    (iv) Documentation of configurations and integrations

(b) Vendor shall not impose termination fees if Customer terminates due to:
    (i) Material breach by Vendor
    (ii) Failure to meet SLAs for three consecutive months
    (iii) Security incident affecting Customer Data

---

### SLA with Teeth Clause

---

**8.2 Service Level Credits**

(a) Service level credits shall be automatically applied to the next invoice without requiring Customer claim.

(b) Credit calculation:

| Availability | Monthly Credit |
|--------------|----------------|
| 99.5% - 99.9% | 10% of monthly fee |
| 99.0% - 99.5% | 25% of monthly fee |
| 95.0% - 99.0% | 50% of monthly fee |
| Below 95.0% | 100% of monthly fee |

(c) If availability falls below 99.0% for three consecutive months, Customer may terminate without penalty.

(d) Credits shall not be capped at less than 100% of monthly fees.

---
```
